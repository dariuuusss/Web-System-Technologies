Addressing mental fatigue experienced by College students


Introduction

Addressing mental fatigue among college students is crucial for their well-being and academic success. Strategies include promoting healthy habits, providing support services like counseling, fostering a supportive campus culture, and encouraging faculty flexibility. These efforts can help students manage stress and thrive in their academic pursuits.

Issues leading to mental fatigue


Academic Stress

Academic pressure is “the tension, discomfort, and other emotions caused by the pressure from school, family, and society in the learning process.” The phenomenon of academic pressure among college students has become more and more common in our daily lives. Some college students believed that peer pressure had harmed them, and had a negative impact. This section analyzed the influencing factors of academic pressure on college students, such as gender, the family environment, and grades, and the methods for college students to reduce academic pressure.

Financial Struggle

Financial issues in college, such as tuition fees and living expenses, often cause significant stress for students. The pressure to manage expenses can lead to mental fatigue as students worry about debt, work part-time jobs, and struggle to afford necessities. This financial strain can hinder academic focus and contribute to overall mental exhaustion.
